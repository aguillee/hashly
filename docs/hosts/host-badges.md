# 🎖️ Attendance Badges for Hosts

Event hosts can create and distribute **attendance NFT badges** to prove participation in their events.

---

## 🛠️ How to Create a Badge

1. 📅 Submit a meetup event and get it approved.
2. 📩 Request host permissions from the admin team.
3. ✅ Once approved as a host, create your badge:

| Field | Example |
|---|---|
| **Badge name** | "Hedera Meetup Madrid - April 2026" |
| **Description** | What the badge represents |
| **Image** | Visual design for the badge NFT |

4. 🪙 The badge NFT token is created on Hedera.

---

## 📱 Event Day: Check-in

### 🔲 QR Code

A **unique QR code** is generated for your event. Display it:

- 🖥️ On a screen at the event venue
- 📽️ On a projector
- 💬 Shared in the event chat (for online events)

### ⚙️ How Check-in Works

1. 📷 Attendees scan the QR code with their phone.
2. 🔢 They enter the **time-based code** displayed alongside the QR.
3. ✅ The check-in is verified and recorded on HCS.
4. 📋 Attendees appear on your check-in list.

> ⏰ **Note:** The check-in code rotates periodically to prevent sharing with people who are not present.

---

## 🎁 Badge Distribution

After the event:

1. 📋 Review your list of checked-in attendees.
2. 🚀 Initiate the badge airdrop to all checked-in wallets.
3. 🪙 Badges are minted and sent to attendees' wallets.
4. 🔄 You have up to **3 airdrop attempts** before the badge expires.

---

## 💎 Badge Impact

Badges are important for your attendees because:

- 🏆 They earn **badge points** that count toward the season leaderboard.
- 🎯 **Badge missions:** having 1 badge = **300 XP**, having 3 badges = **1,000 XP**.
- 🔗 Badges serve as **verifiable proof of attendance**, forever on-chain.
